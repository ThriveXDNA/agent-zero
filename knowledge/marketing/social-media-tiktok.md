## **PART 1: TIKTOK ALGORITHM - UNDERSTANDING WHAT WORKS**

### **1.1 How TikTok\'s Algorithm Actually Works**

TikTok\'s algorithm is the most powerful discovery engine in social
media. Unlike other platforms that prioritize existing followers, TikTok
shows your content to strangers---meaning anyone can go viral regardless
of follower count.

The Core Ranking Factors for TikTok (Ranked by Importance):

\| Priority \| Signal \| Why It Matters \|

\|\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\--\|\-\-\-\-\-\-\--\|\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\--\|

\| 1 (Highest) \| Watch Time \| Total time spent watching matters more
than likes. If people watch your entire video, TikTok pushes it to more
people. \|

\| 2 \| Rewatches \| If people watch your video MORE THAN ONCE, it\'s a
massive engagement signal. This is the #1 predictor of viral content. \|

\| 3 \| Engagement (Likes, Comments, Shares) \| Comments and shares
signal that content is worth spreading. Shares are the strongest
engagement signal. \|

\| 4 \| Video Information \| Captions, sounds, and effects help TikTok
understand and categorize your content for the \"For You\" page. \|

\| 5 (Lowest) \| Device & Account Settings \| TikTok considers language,
location, and device type to optimize content relevance. \|

The TikTok Discovery Loop:

TikTok\'s algorithm tests content by showing it to a small group of
viewers (typically 100-500). If this group engages positively (watch
time, likes, comments, shares), TikTok shows it to a larger group
(1,000-10,000). This process continues exponentially if engagement stays
high.

The First Hour Rule:

The first hour after posting is critical. TikTok\'s algorithm determines
initial distribution within the first 30-60 minutes. Strong early
engagement triggers larger distribution.

### **1.2 How TikTok Differs from YouTube Shorts**

While TikTok and YouTube Shorts share similarities, key differences
exist:

\| Factor \| TikTok \| YouTube Shorts \|

\|\-\-\-\-\-\-\--\|\-\-\-\-\-\-\--\|\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\-\--\|

\| Algorithm Speed \| Faster initial distribution \| Slower, more
gradual \|

\| Discovery Rate \| Higher for new accounts \| More weight on existing
subscribers \|

\| Audio Library \| Larger, more trending sounds \| YouTube Audio
Library \|

\| Video Length \| Up to 10 minutes (longer allowed) \| Maximum 60
seconds \|

\| Content Style \| More raw, authentic \| Can be more polished \|

\| Hashtag Discovery \| Stronger hashtag discovery \| Less discovery via
hashtags \|

## **PART 3: POSTING BEST PRACTICES**

### **3.1 Posting Schedule**

Optimal Posting Times:

\- Morning: 7:00 AM - 9:00 AM CT (commute audience)

\- Midday: 12:00 PM - 2:00 PM CT (lunch break browsing)

\- Evening: 6:00 PM - 9:00 PM CT (prime time)

\- Late Night: 9:00 PM - 11:00 PM CT (wind-down browsing)

Best Days:

\- Tuesday through Thursday (highest engagement)

\- Monday and Friday (moderate)

\- Weekends (variable---can be good but competition lower)

Posting Frequency:

\- Minimum: 1 video per day

\- Ideal: 3-5 videos per day

\- Maximum: 10 videos per day (diminishing returns)

Consistency is Key:

Posting at the same time daily helps your audience know when to expect
content. This builds routine viewing habits.

### **3.2 Caption Best Practices**

Caption Structure:

Hook (first line - most important)

\-

Body (context, value)

\-

CTA (comment, share, follow)

\-

#hashtags

Caption Rules:

\- First line must hook (it\'s visible before \"more\")

\- Keep captions conversational

\- Use line breaks for readability

\- Include CTAs naturally

\- Maximum 2,200 characters (but shorter is better)

Engagement Triggers:

\- Ask questions at end

\- Use \"Comment below\...\"

\- Say \"Share this if\...\"

\- Use \"Tag a friend who needs to see this\"

### **3.3 Video Descriptions & Tags**

Description Tips:

\- Keep descriptions under 150 characters for best visibility

\- Add relevant keywords naturally

\- Include context that adds value

Video Tags:

\- TikTok doesn\'t use tags like YouTube

\- Focus on sounds and hashtags instead

\- Choose sounds that categorize your content

## **PART 4: ENGAGEMENT RULES**

### **4.1 Comment Management**

Response Time Requirements:

\- First hour: Monitor continuously

\- Within 4 hours: Respond to all comments

\- Within 24 hours: Reply to every single comment

Why Early Replies Matter:

TikTok\'s algorithm prioritizes content with active engagement. Quick
responses encourage more comments, which triggers broader distribution.

Comment Response Guidelines:

\- Respond to questions with helpful answers

\- Reply with video responses for popular questions

\- Use humor when appropriate

\- Never argue with negative commenters---ignore or hide

### **4.2 Engagement Strategies**

Within Your Videos:

\- End with a question asking for comments

\- Use text overlays: \"Comment your answer\"

\- Create debate-worthy content

\- Leave things incomplete to encourage discussion

Outside Your Videos:

\- Reply to comments on your own videos (first)

\- Comment on other creators\' videos (daily)

\- Duet or Stitch relevant content

\- Engage with your niche community

### **4.3 Community Building**

Daily Engagement Tasks:

\- Comment on 10-15 relevant creator videos

\- Reply to all comments on your videos

\- Respond to DMs within 24 hours

\- Engage with trending content in your niche

Building Followers:

\- Post consistently (daily minimum)

\- Respond to all comments

\- Create series content (people return for more)

\- Use consistent branding and voice

## **PART 7: VIDEO PRODUCTION WORKFLOW**

### **7.1 Creating TikTok Content (Reference to Main Guide)**

For detailed video production steps, see
\`VIDEOPRODUCTIONCHECKLISTWITHWHY.md\`. Below is the abbreviated
workflow for TikTok:

Phase 1: Research (15-30 min)

1\. Check TikTok Discover page for trending sounds

2\. Identify trending formats in your niche

3\. Research what top creators are posting

4\. Validate your topic idea

Phase 2: Concept Development (15 min)

1\. Choose a trending sound (or create original)

2\. Design your hook (first 1-2 seconds)

3\. Plan the content structure

4\. Write script if needed (short, conversational)

Phase 3: Production (30-60 min)

1\. Record A-roll (on-camera, high energy)

2\. Record B-roll (supporting visuals)

3\. Record multiple takes

4\. Capture trending sound or voiceover

Phase 4: Editing (30-60 min)

1\. Cut to optimal length (15-45 seconds)

2\. Add captions (every word)

3\. Add text overlays

4\. Sync to trending audio

5\. Add transitions and effects

6\. Ensure video loops naturally (if applicable)

Phase 5: Upload & Optimize

1\. Write compelling caption with hook

2\. Add relevant hashtags (3-5)

3\. Select appropriate sound

4\. Post at optimal times

### **7.2 Duet & Stitch Strategy**

Duets:

\- React to other creators\' content

\- Add your perspective or value

\- Use for collaboration requests

Stitches:

\- Use other videos as setup for your content

\- Add your unique take

\- Great for responding to questions or debates

Best Practices:

\- Always add value beyond the original

\- Give credit to original creator

\- Use for engagement, not just views
